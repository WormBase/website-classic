C. elegans Cell Report for HOB

Summary

HOB is a male-specific sensory neuron, whose cell body is located in the pre-anal ganglion, and which is necessary for male sensation of his partner during mating behavior.

Physical Features

Morphology: Single dendrite. Axon is (Sulston et al. 1980). Figure 23 in Sulston et al. 1980 . Cell body has dilated cisternae similar to RnB cells.
Location: Pre-anal ganglion
Time/stage/sex: L4 Male
Development: HOB is P10.ppap, generated postembryonically from P10.p, the secondary pre-anal ganglion

Field Guide: The single HOB cell body is located just left of center in the pre-anal ganglion, with its dendrite extending into the hook sensillum. HOB is posterior to HOA. lov-1:GFP fusion identifies the cell, Barr & Sternberg, 1999

Functional Features

Ablation: Males fail to locate the vulva (Liu & Sternberg, 1995); (Sulston & White, 1980) Males lacking sensory rays fail to respond to hermaphrodites (Liu & Sternberg, 1995
Related phenotypes: Location of vulva defective (Lov)
